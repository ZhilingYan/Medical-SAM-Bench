Abstract

SAMed2 represents a breakthrough in medical image segmentation by adapting the Segment Anything Model 2 (SAM2) for the medical domain. Our approach introduces learnable medical prompts and cross-scale consistency mechanisms that enable superior performance across diverse medical imaging modalities.

Learnable Medical Prompts

We introduce a memory bank of 640 learnable prompts specifically designed for medical imaging tasks, enabling the model to capture domain-specific patterns.

Cross-Scale Consistency

Our novel cross-scale consistency mechanism ensures robust segmentation across different image resolutions and modalities.

State-of-the-Art Performance

SAMed2 achieves an average Dice score of 92.3% across 18 medical imaging benchmarks, outperforming previous methods by a significant margin.

Open Source

We release our code, models, and the MedBank dataset to facilitate reproducibility and advance medical image segmentation research.

Method

SAMed2 builds upon SAM2's architecture with three key innovations:

  • Medical Prompt Memory Bank: A learnable set of 640 prompts that encode medical domain knowledge
  • Cross-Scale Feature Fusion: Multi-scale feature aggregation for handling varying organ sizes
  • Modality-Aware Encoding: Adaptive encoding strategies for different imaging modalities
